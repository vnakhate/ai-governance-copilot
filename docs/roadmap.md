## Vision

### The reframe (premise challenge)
The demo answers "can I use this?" questions. But the business outcome is *reducing
ungoverned AI usage* (223 shadow-AI incidents/month per org; $4.63M avg breach). A
reactive copilot only reaches the conscientious minority who ask — and shadow AI exists
precisely because most people don't ask. So a standalone copilot optimizes a proxy
(answered questions), not the outcome (complete AI inventory + less ungoverned use).

### 10x check / North Star B — Governance engine, many surfaces
The real product is the **verdict + capture engine** (`/assess` + `/capture`), with chat
as client #1. The same engine is callable from every point an AI-use decision happens:
Glean chat, a headless API, a browser/paste-time interception, Slack, procurement intake,
CI for internally-built AI. Vanta holds a live, eventually auto-discovered AI inventory.
The product is governance infrastructure, not a chatbot. Chat is one surface.

## Accepted Scope (added to the demo)
- **Dual-surface beat (E2):** after the chat verdict, call `/assess` headlessly and show the
  identical verdict + Vanta record. Turns "it's a chatbot" into "it's an engine with a chat client."
- **Audit-every-assessment (F1):** append every `/assess` to an append-only JSONL audit log
  ({ts, who, tool, data, jurisdiction, verdict}). `proceeded` is set true when `/capture`
  later fires for that decision; otherwise it stays `null` (the demo does not wire a
  reply-level "no" signal — that's roadmap). **On stage:** show the audit file tailing live
  as questions are asked ("a complete record of every AI-use decision, even the ones nobody
  acted on"). A true tamper-evident store with RBAC + retention is deferred to roadmap.
